About

I received a B.A. in Economics, with a minor in Mathematics, from National Taiwan University and am currently enrolled in the Master in Mathematics and Economic Decision at Toulouse School of Economics.

My research interests include applied microeconomics, labor economics, and empirical methods. I am particularly interested in questions related to household decision-making, inequality, and policy evaluation.

Research Interests: Applied Microeconomics, Labor Economics, Household Economics, Empirical Methods.

Research

Bachelor Thesis: Wealth and Assortative Mating in Taiwanese Marriages

National Taiwan University, 2024-2025

Examines assortative matching on wealth in Taiwanese marriages using administrative tax data, with emphasis on intergenerational wealth and household-level implications.

Individual Behavior in LUPI Game: Evidence from Laboratory and Field Experiments

Research Assistant, NTU Economics, 2024-2025

Contributed to empirical analysis of large-scale field experiments and investigated behavioral learning dynamics in winner-takes-all environments.
